Konteks Masalah

The Harmonization Gap in Public Sector Data

The core issue is not a lack of data, but the absence of shared meaning behind it. Indonesia's Satu Data initiative faces critical fragmentation challenges.

Fragmented Sources

Indicators and metadata scattered across policy documents, spreadsheets, statistical reports, and legacy systems with no unified structure.

Inconsistent Meaning

Definitions, units, methodologies, update cycles, and data ownership vary significantly across institutions and jurisdictions.

Manual Harmonization

Metadata review still requires layered coordination between data producers, walidata, and pembina — a slow, error-prone process.

Analisis AI

Why Simple AI Is Not Enough

Extraction, LLMs, and RAG help read data — but they are insufficient for true harmonization across government institutions.

Extraction

Text, tables, indicators, metadata

Limitation: Cannot understand relationships between indicators, definition conflicts, or institutional context.

LLM

Summarize, explain, compare

Limitation: Answers can be inconsistent, difficult to audit, and not designed for cross-institutional decisions.

RAG

Retrieve relevant information

Limitation: Helps retrieval but does not build shared knowledge structures or resolve conflicts.
The Metagraph Difference

Knowledge Alignment Engine

Simple AI solves access to information. Metagraph aligns meaning for harmonization. Public-sector data harmonization requires more than reading documents — it demands relationships, reasoning, and accountable decisions.

Kebutuhan Harmonisasi

What Harmonization Actually Needs

A system that doesn't just read documents, but builds context, reasons through conflicts, and supports accountable decisions.

01

Structured Extraction

Transform documents into structured metadata — indicators, definitions, units, institutions — from PDF, Word, Excel, and policy documents using AI-powered parsing.

02

Semantic Context

Build relationships between data elements: Programs, Indicators, Datasets, Definitions, and Institutions — so the system understands context, not just text.

03

Deliberative Reasoning

Assess similarity, conflicts, and ambiguity across perspectives through rule-based checks, semantic matching, graph reasoning, and multi-agent discussion.

04

Accountable Decision-Making

Keep decisions auditable through human validation at critical decision points and continuous learning from review outcomes.
